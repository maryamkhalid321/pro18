ego and psychology In psychology, the ego is a key concept in Sigmund Freud's theory of personality. It's the part of the mind that mediates between our primal desires (the id), our moral conscience (the superego), and reality1. The ego operates based on the reality principle, working to satisfy the id's desires in a realistic and socially appropriate manner. The ego uses defense mechanisms like denial, repression, and rationalization to manage conflicts between the id and superego, and to protect us from anxiety and distress. Essentially, the ego helps us navigate the complexities of life by balancing our inner desires with external demands
